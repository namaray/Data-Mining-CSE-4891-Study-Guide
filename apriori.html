Association Rule Mining (ARM) & Apriori

Association Rule Mining is a data mining technique used to discover interesting relationships or patterns within large datasets. It is most famous for "Market Basket Analysis" (e.g., finding out that people who buy bread also buy butter).

1. Key Metrics

To determine if a relationship is strong, we use two measurements:

1. Support (Frequency)

Measures how often items appear together.

"Is this combination popular?"

2. Confidence (Reliability)

Measures the likelihood that Item B is bought if Item A is bought.

"How strong is the relationship?"

2. The Problem & Solution

The Brute Force Approach: Trying to calculate every possible combination of items is computationally prohibited. It takes too much computer power.

The Solution (Apriori Principle):

"If an itemset is infrequent, all its supersets are also infrequent."

This means if people rarely buy Pickles, we don't need to waste time checking if they buy Pickles & Ice Cream.

Step 3 (Final Scan)

Merge surviving pairs into triplets.

Note: We don't check {A, B, C} because {A, B} was pruned in Step 2.

4. Real-World Applications

  • Market Basket Analysis: Product placement and cross-selling (e.g., Bread & Butter).
  • Recommendation Systems: Netflix/Amazon suggestions ("Users who bought X also bought Y").
  • Healthcare Analytics: Correlating patient symptoms with effective treatments.
  • Fraud Detection: Identifying unusual transaction sequences that don't match standard rules.
